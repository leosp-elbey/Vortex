## 1. Purpose

This skill is a **worldwide travel-demand radar and campaign generator**. It exists to help VortexTrips capture demand *before* it peaks by turning predictable real-world events (carnivals, sports finals, festivals, conferences, cruises, family reunions, weddings, holidays) into ready-to-publish marketing campaigns.

What it does conceptually:
- Watches global event calendars and travel-intent windows.
- Scores each event for VortexTrips fit (commission, urgency, audience, social potential).
- Generates a complete campaign package — copy, posts, emails, DMs, image/video prompts, and a posting schedule.
- Routes to the existing VortexTrips automation stack (OpenRouter cheap model for drafts, Claude for verification, content_calendar for scheduling).

What it is **not**:
- It is not a deployable service. Phase 14A is the spec only.
- It is not a replacement for the existing weekly-content cron — it sits on top of it as an event-aware overlay.
- It does not bypass human approval. Every generated campaign lands as a draft.

## 2. Core Campaign Formula

Every campaign produced by this skill must resolve this six-part formula:

```
Destination + Event + Audience + Travel Window + Savings Angle + CTA
```

- **Destination** — the city or region where the event happens (Trinidad, Miami, Doha, Cancun, etc.).
- **Event** — the specific demand spike (Carnival, World Cup match, Art Basel, family reunion).
- **Audience** — who travels for it (diaspora, sports fans, faith group, alumni, parents).
- **Travel Window** — when bookings actually happen for this event (180-day to 7-day waves).
- **Savings Angle** — how a VortexTrips member rate may help (hotel, cruise, flight, group block).
- **CTA** — the single next step (claim free portal at `myvortex365.com/leosp`, enroll at `signup.surge365.com/leosp`, or DM "rate" for a member quote).

If any of the six is missing, the campaign is incomplete.

## 4. Event Timing Logic

Campaigns ship in eight pre-defined waves, anchored to the event's start date. Each wave has its own dominant emotion and CTA tone.

| Wave | Window | Emotion | Dominant CTA |
|---|---|---|---|
| **W1 — Awareness** | 180 days before | "Have you heard?" | Save the date, capture lead |
| **W2 — Planning** | 120 days before | "Time to plan" | Member rate quote, lock hotel |
| **W3 — Hotel Pressure** | 90 days before | "Rates are climbing" | Member rate vs. public rate |
| **W4 — Urgency** | 60 days before | "It's filling up" | Book group block, last good rooms |
| **W5 — Last Call** | 30 days before | "This is it" | Final pricing snapshot |
| **W6 — Nearby Hotels** | 14 days before | "Sold out at the venue?" | Overflow/satellite hotel options |
| **W7 — Last-Minute** | 7 days before | "Going anyway?" | Last-minute member rates, ground transfer |
| **W8 — Next Year Waitlist** | Post-event | "Don't miss it again" | Capture lead for next year early-bird list |

Each wave may produce 2-5 social posts plus 1 email. The full eight-wave program for one event = ~20-35 pieces of content over ~6 months.

## 6. Cruise Add-On Logic

When `event_type` includes Cruise Travel, the campaign must additionally specify:

- **departure_city** — port city (Miami, Galveston, Seattle, etc.)
- **cruise_line** — if known (Royal Caribbean, Carnival, MSC, Disney, Norwegian, Holland America)
- **pre_cruise_hotel** — 1-2 night option in the departure city
- **post_cruise_hotel** — 1-2 night decompression stay
- **group_cruise_angle** — bachelor/bachelorette, family reunion, alumni, networking
- **family_cruise_angle** — kid clubs, adjoining cabins, dining
- **singles_cruise_angle** — solo/single supplement waivers, mixers
- **gospel_cruise_angle** — faith-based group rates and itineraries
- **luxury_cruise_angle** — suite class, butler service, premium drink package
- **closed_loop_angle** — for U.S. residents without a passport: "passport-friendly closed-loop sailing departing from a U.S. port"

Cruise campaigns should always pair the cruise with a 1-night pre-cruise hotel as a default upsell.

## 7. Compliance Rules (HARD)

These rules are non-negotiable. The skill must reject or rewrite any draft that violates them.

- **Never guarantee income.** No "you will earn $X." Use "members report," "earnings vary," or "see income disclosure."
- **Never guarantee savings unless verified.** No "save 60% guaranteed." Use:
  - "may save"
  - "members report savings on hotels and cruises"
  - "check your member rate"
  - "see if your rate is lower than public pricing"
- **Tax / business travel:** always close with "ask your tax professional." Never state tax outcomes.
- **No unsupported claims** about active member counts, total dollars saved, star ratings, or guaranteed results.
- **Banned vocabulary:** MLM, downline, network marketing.
- **Required vocabulary:** travel membership, affiliate program, travel savings club.
- **Health / wellness:** wellness retreat copy must avoid medical claims.
- **No celebrity endorsements** unless explicitly licensed.
- **No copyrighted event imagery** — use Pexels, licensed art, or generic event-style imagery with text overlay.

## 8. Automation Rules

The skill is designed to be lifted into code in later phases (see `EVENT_CAMPAIGN_ROADMAP.md`). When automated, it must:

- Run on a **weekly cadence** (one cron tick per week is enough; events are slow-moving).
- **Research upcoming events** from approved sources (TBD in Phase 14C — initial seed list will be hand-curated).
- **Score** each candidate against the rubric in §9 and only generate full campaigns for items above a configurable threshold (default: 60/100).
- **Generate drafts** using the OpenRouter cheap-tier model (`AI_CHEAP_MODEL` — currently `meta-llama/llama-3.3-70b-instruct`).
- **Verify** every draft with Claude (`AI_VERIFIER_MODEL`) before insertion. The verifier checks: brand voice, compliance §7, formula §2 completeness, factual sanity.
- **Insert** approved drafts into `content_calendar` with `status='draft'` and `requires_human_approval=true`.
- **Source images** from Pexels (existing integration) or generate via OpenAI when Pexels has no fit.
- **Log** every run to `ai_jobs` and `ai_model_usage` for budget tracking.
- **Respect budget guards** — `AI_DAILY_BUDGET_LIMIT` and `AI_MONTHLY_BUDGET_LIMIT` already enforced in `ai-router.ts`. No new budget logic needed.

## 9. Campaign Scoring (1-100)

Each candidate event/campaign is scored on ten dimensions, weighted as follows. The total is 0-100.

| # | Dimension | Weight | What it measures |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Travel demand | 15 | Volume of people who travel for this event |
| 2 | Hotel price pressure | 12 | Likelihood of inventory crunch / rate spike |
| 3 | Group travel likelihood | 10 | Friends, family, alumni, faith — multi-room potential |
| 4 | Audience buying intent | 12 | Will the audience actually pay to travel, not just talk? |
| 5 | Social content potential | 10 | Visual appeal; shareability |
| 6 | Commission potential | 12 | Total trip value × commission rate × take rate |
| 7 | Urgency | 8 | Time pressure baked into the calendar |
| 8 | Competition level | 6 | Inverse weight — more affiliates competing = lower score |
| 9 | Cruise / hotel add-on | 8 | Can we sell a multi-product trip? |
| 10 | Repeatability next year | 7 | Will this event recur and let us harvest the audience again? |

A score ≥ 70 = priority queue (build full 8-wave program). 60-69 = standard queue (build 4 waves). < 60 = parking lot (re-evaluate next quarter).
